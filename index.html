🛂 Border Crossing Tips
Which crossing
Use San Ysidro (I-5 south) — largest, 24/7, most lanes. Otay Mesa (SR-905) is faster midday on weekdays but adds miles to your route. For a 6:30 AM Friday departure, San Ysidro is fine — wait times are minimal that early.
Southbound (US → Mexico)
No stopping required. Drive through. No passport check going south — just look for the green light lane and roll through. Keep insurance docs accessible but they rarely ask.
Northbound (Mexico → US)
This is where the wait is. Budget 30–90 min on Monday morning. SENTRI/Ready lane cuts it to 5–10 min if you have it. Otherwise standard lane. Have passports, vehicle registration, and be ready to declare any items (food, alcohol, tobacco).
Vehicle permit
Baja California does NOT require a temporary vehicle import permit (TIP) — only applies if you're going to mainland Mexico. No permit needed for this trip.
Fuel into Mexico
Currently no official restriction on fuel carried in the tank. Some checkpoints may ask about extra gas cans — be honest, they're common for off-road use and rarely an issue. Keep cans properly sealed and vented.
Returning with the KTM
Declare the motorcycle. It came from the US so no duty, but have your title or registration. If asked, it's your personal recreational vehicle being re-imported — you took it to Mexico for a race event.
